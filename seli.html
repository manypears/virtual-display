Gardening the Digital: Curating Belonging, Metadata, and Fog in a Virtual Landscape

Selihamashang Kutame

This virtual display project extends my ongoing research on digital belongings, dematerialisation, and the conditions of online circulation. The broader research examines how digital images, files, and memes function as cultural artefacts that appear weightless yet carry intimate social, emotional, and historical meaning. Following Marshall McLuhan’s assertion that “the medium is the message” (McLuhan 1964), I argue that the digital environment conditions how we perceive, store, and share these objects. Their dematerialisation on the internet is not simply a technical property but a cultural transformation: digital objects lose their sense of origin, ownership, and material integrity as they circulate.
