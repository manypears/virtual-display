Remnant / Resonant

Lieza Campbell

My virtual curation project titled Remnant/Resonant imagines a vault that preserves the soundscapes of endangered ecosystems in South Africa, in a dystopian future where these natural sounds are fading or have completely vanished. In this instance, the vault accommodates two biomes: the Succulent Karoo and the Marine biome. Serving as a transitional space between the two biomes is a foyer that can be accessed through an elevator. In each room, there is a holographic display that presents information of the vault and its biomes.

This virtual curation project functions as a speculative memorial and an archive. Furthermore, it explores the fragility of ecological memory and how digital media can both memorialise and mourn the loss of sonic ecologies. The design of the vault is inspired by eco-brutalism architecture, where the confined, concrete structures juxtapose with the organic elements such as the flora, water, sunlight and natural sounds. Each biome consists of reconstructed soundscapes, proposing a speculative archive for fading or already lost sonic ecologies in South Africa. Through fabricating soundscapes instead of recording, the project explores the role of digital media in creating a speculative narrative where these soundscapes are remnants of the sonic ecologies which have vanished in a dystopian future.
