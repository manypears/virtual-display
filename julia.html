Tell us about yourself

Julia Leisegang

Crafting is an activity of creation that forms communities, connection and intimacy. It has origins within the domestic space, and although this origin can be considered as one of restriction and confinement, the act of crafting exists beyond this confinement of domesticity. Even if crafts such as crochet and knitting are associated with the domestic space and exist as domestic activities the communities and intimacy that it provides in connection between crafters allows it to transcend the attempt to confine it. The space of the craft circle enables communities and connections to flow and for people to share their stories in a space of crafting. It is not just the act of crafting and the people who craft that hold intimacy, these spaces of care and intimacy produce items that can hold the same intimacy through an understanding of the life it has been given through crafting. The intimacy it holds becomes a signifier of those who it has gone through life with. In this project I explore how these objects hold life, intimacy and connection through their memories and experiences, much like the intimacy that is provided to people through craft, and through this intimacy it transcends any attempt to confine and restrict its existence.
