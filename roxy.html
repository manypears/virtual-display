Making Stage

Roxanne Rose Modricky

Making Stage is an exploration of time, repetition and rehearsal curated for the digital space. Placing the viewer inside of a Zoetrope made up of six different stages, or ‘scenes,’ that spins slowly revealing the process of rehearsal. I have used Shakespeare’s Sonnet 29 as an object to capture the progress of the rehearsal process and to attempt to materialise the time and labour needed to create a performance. The repetition of this text in the space acts as both a process of illumination and as a metaphor, revealing how, through repetition, meaning becomes clearer. The chair serves as an ‘actor, (referred to in the essay as a ‘chair-actor,’) marking the passage of time across the stages visible to the audience. The sixth stage represents the final performance; where there is a single layer of text that illuminates (and obscures) the chair and stage.
