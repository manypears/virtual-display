Moesha Mdhluli

The project explores the question: what does it mean to make a house that is quite a home? A building may be considered a house; however, a home is something else within the physical structure of a house. This project specifically examines the performance of home in digital spaces.

The conceptualization of the project came about as I was searching for visual references of Black domestic architecture. The results were unhelpful, showing instead a plethora of AI-generated “modern” interior spaces or linking to websites and marketplaces for Black-contemporary-cultural interior finishing. This led me to think about how the experience and performance of home manifest in digital spaces, specifically in relation to care, ownership, and aesthetic practices.

The model of a domestic space is used to foreground communal homemaking practices by drawing attention to the entryway, living, and dining room. There is a specificity in rendering an experiential navigation of the home by using recognizable objects, immaterial objects (through shadows), and emanating color. The focus on communal sites and their practices alludes to the shared relationship that digital space has with the domestic—in terms of social construction and mechanisms.
