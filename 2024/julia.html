Julia Snowball

My virtual curation explores the work of two xingwei artists, He Yunchang and Gu Dexin. Xingwei, zhuangzhi, and yishu—often referred to simply as xingwei—are similar sub-genres of Chinese performance art that make use of “flesh as medium,” be that the body of the artist, cadavers, or animals. These performances were ephemeral in nature, leaving behind little evidence that they existed aside from a few photographs, which are scarce as well. My curation attempts to make a more “permanent” record of these pieces and the artists’ careers, while simultaneously exploring what happens to these works and how they are changed when transformed into the virtual space.

The distinct lack of flesh or bodies questions the centrality of abjection to the movement and explores the idea of skeuomorphism in the virtual space. While parts of He’s performances are arranged next to one another in chronological order, Gu’s are merged into one, representing the fragility of memory and the risk of these under-documented works disappearing, as they exist largely in the minds of the audience members.

Ultimately, my curation is concerned with the intersection of performance art and virtuality and is a study on the effectiveness of these spaces in portraying movements such as xingwei.
