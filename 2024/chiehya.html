Ho Chieh-Ya


This virtual curation aims to narrate the history of my research object, a Chinese porcelain bowl. Five bowls, each differ in size and design, are arranged on a round table. These include the model of the original bowl, one featuring a shipwreck image, two showcasing images of the bowl’s Iziko Museum accession cards, and one model of my attempt to replicate the original bowl. The table, accompanied by five chairs, invites viewers to sit and engage more closely with the bowls. Moving away from the usual method of displaying porcelain on individual stands or behind glass cases, the round table evokes a dining scene, connecting the bowls to their functional purpose as well as referencing their Asian origins and dining customs.

Surrounding the room are pictures that capture moments inthe bowl’s story. Each bowl is paired with its corresponding images on the opposite wall. When viewers sit in front of one of the five bowls, they are positioned to see the associated images, establishing a connection between the bowl and its narrative.

The virtual environment is designed as an immersive space. By distorting and deconstructing the bowl’s design (outer floral patterns and inner circle ring) and projecting it onto the walls, floor, and ceiling ofthe room, the viewer is given the sensation of being inside the bowl itself.
